# GTM.md — Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact target user

Not "startups" — that's too broad.

The primary user is an **engineering manager or CTO at a 5–20 person startup**,
Series A or pre-Series A, who:
- Is personally responsible for the engineering budget
- Has approved at least 3–4 AI tool subscriptions over the past year
- Has never audited them because there was no tool to do it
- Gets a Stripe or credit card bill every month and mentally notes "that seems
  high" but doesn't act on it

Secondary user: a **solo founder or indie developer** who pays for multiple AI
tools personally and suspects they're overlapping but hasn't done the math.

The person who is NOT the target: an individual developer at a large company
where someone else controls the budget. They have no reason to act on savings
they don't personally feel.

## How to get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0

### Week 1 — Seed with a real story
Post on Hacker News "Show HN: I built a free AI subscription auditor after
our team's bill hit $800/mo." Real numbers, real context. HN loves this format.
Target: 200–500 upvotes, 20–50 direct signups from comments alone.

### Week 2 — Reddit seeding
Post the audit results screenshot (high savings case) to:
- r/ExperiencedDevs: "Audited our AI stack, found $340/mo in overlap"
- r/SideProject: "Built a free tool to find AI subscription waste"
- r/ChatGPT and r/ClaudeAI: "Made something that tells you if you're
  overpaying for this"

No "check out my tool" framing. Lead with the insight, link as the source.

### Week 3 — Twitter/X #buildinpublic thread
Screenshot the most dramatic audit result (Cursor Ultra + Claude overlap).
Thread: "Most teams using Cursor Ultra don't need it. Here's the math."
Tag relevant accounts: @levelsio, @marc_louvion, developer tooling creators.

### Week 4 — Direct outreach
Find 20 Indie Hackers who have publicly posted their monthly expenses
(many do this in "monthly update" posts). DM them: "I built something that
audits exactly this — would you try it and tell me what's wrong with it?"
This gets both users and feedback.
